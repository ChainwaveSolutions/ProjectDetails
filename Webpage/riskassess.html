Introduction

As part of Chainwave Solutions’ commitment to preventing money laundering, terrorist financing, and other illicit financial activities, a comprehensive risk assessment will be conducted regularly to evaluate potential AML risks associated with our on-ramp/off-ramp services, network bridge, and transactions involving both Web3 and fiat systems.

Scope

This AML risk assessment covers:

  • On-ramp: Converting fiat currency to cryptocurrency for users and businesses.
  • Off-ramp: Converting cryptocurrency back to fiat currency.
  • Network bridge: Facilitating cross-chain and cross-network transactions for payments of goods, services, and financial transactions involving both Web3 and traditional fiat systems.

Risk Factors Considered

1. Transaction Complexity

Transactions involving cryptocurrency on Web3 and conversions between fiat and crypto present heightened complexity, increasing AML risks. Complex transaction chains can obscure the flow of funds and create opportunities for money laundering.

2. Cross-Border Transactions

Cross-border transactions introduce jurisdictional challenges. AML laws vary by country, leading to potential gaps in oversight. Payments through network bridges may involve jurisdictions with less stringent AML enforcement.

3. Anonymity of Transactions

Cryptocurrencies can offer anonymity, particularly when using privacy-focused coins or decentralized exchanges (DEXs). This makes it difficult to verify the origin of funds, increasing the risk of facilitating illicit activities.

4. Nature of Business Transactions

Web3 services may cater to users with limited financial transparency. These services could potentially be exploited by criminals to conduct high-risk transactions such as using crypto assets to settle illegal goods or services.

5. Customer and Counterparty Risk

Businesses that engage in high-value transactions with limited transparency present higher risks. The integration of fiat systems introduces additional risk if customers or counterparties are located in high-risk jurisdictions.

6. Layering and Structuring Risks

The integration of on-ramp/off-ramp services and cross-network bridges could enable users to split large transactions into smaller, less detectable amounts or move funds across multiple layers to avoid detection.

7. Technology Risks

The use of decentralized financial technologies (DeFi) and smart contracts creates risks where traditional AML mechanisms may not apply.

Risk Categories and Mitigation Measures

1. Geographical Risk

High-Risk Jurisdictions: Transactions in countries with weak AML frameworks will be flagged for enhanced due diligence.

Mitigation: Implement automated tools to assess geographical risk and screen for high-risk jurisdictions.

2. Customer Risk

High-Risk Customers: Anonymous transactions or customers with inconsistent patterns will be considered higher risk.

Mitigation: Implement CDD and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) processes to verify customer identities and transactions.

3. Transaction Risk

Large/Unusual Transactions: High-value or frequent transactions in low-transparency markets may indicate attempts to launder funds.

Mitigation: Establish monitoring systems to flag unusual transaction volumes and patterns.

4. Product and Service Risk

Risky Products: Network bridges or DEXs where KYC requirements may not be consistently applied pose higher risks.

Mitigation: Limit high-risk product offerings or ensure additional AML controls for decentralized transactions.

5. Technology Risk

DeFi and Smart Contract Vulnerabilities: The reliance on smart contracts increases the risk of exploitation by bad actors.

Mitigation: Implement regular audits of smart contracts and ensure compliance monitoring for DeFi products.

6. Fiat-to-Crypto and Crypto-to-Fiat Risk

Risk of Untraceable Funds: Fiat-crypto on/off-ramps present risks of untraceable funds.

Mitigation: Integrate AML-compliant service providers for fiat-crypto transactions and mandate KYC checks.

Monitoring and Reporting Mechanisms

  • Real-Time Transaction Monitoring: Automated transaction monitoring tools will detect and flag suspicious activities with real-time alerts.
  • Cross-Network Monitoring: Monitor activities across on/off-ramps and network bridges for irregular activity.
  • Suspicious Activity Reporting: The AML Compliance Officer will investigate and file Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) as necessary.

Ongoing Risk Assessment and Adjustments

Chainwave Solutions will conduct periodic reviews of the risk assessment to address changes in regulatory environments, customer profiles, and new threats in the crypto and fiat ecosystems. Adjustments will be made as necessary to enhance the effectiveness of the AML program and mitigate new or emerging risks.
